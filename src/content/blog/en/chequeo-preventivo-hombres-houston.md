# Men's Preventive Checkup in Houston: Guide by Age

Most Hispanic men in Houston only see a doctor when something hurts. The problem is that the diseases that do the most damage — high blood pressure, diabetes, high cholesterol — don't hurt for years. By the time symptoms appear, they've been progressing for a long time.

A preventive checkup is the simplest way to get ahead of them: in a single visit, the essentials get reviewed and you walk out knowing how you're really doing. In this guide we explain what a men's checkup includes, which screenings you need at each age, and how to get one in Houston — **walk-in, no insurance required, with care 100% in Spanish**.

## Why Get Checkups Even If You Feel Fine?

Feeling fine is not the same as being fine. These conditions tend to advance silently:

- **High blood pressure**: causes no discomfort, but damages the heart, kidneys and eyesight over the years.
- **Type 2 diabetes**: can go undiagnosed for years while excess sugar damages nerves, circulation and kidneys.
- **High cholesterol**: you can't feel it, but it slowly clogs the arteries and raises the risk of a heart attack.
- **Prostate problems**: early changes rarely cause clear symptoms.

Catching any of these conditions early completely changes the outlook: they can almost always be controlled with simple treatment and lifestyle changes. Catching them late means complications you will feel — and that cost far more to treat.

## What a Men's Preventive Checkup Includes

At [Clínica Hispana La Caridad](/services/salud-hombre), a men's checkup is tailored to your age and history, but it generally includes:

### Physical Exam and Blood Pressure

A general review with blood pressure, weight, and a conversation about your symptoms, family history and habits. This is where we decide which tests make sense in your case.

### Complete Blood Work

[Blood tests](/services/examenes-sangre) are the foundation of the checkup: glucose, cholesterol and triglycerides, complete blood count, kidney and liver function. We can also check your [thyroid](/services/tiroides) if there's fatigue or unexplained weight change. Results are ready the same day, so you don't have to come back another day just to learn your numbers.

### Electrocardiogram

An [electrocardiogram](/services/electrocardiograma) checks your heart's rhythm and electrical activity in minutes. It's especially recommended after 40, if heart problems run in your family, or if your job is physically demanding.

### Prostate and Testosterone

Starting at age 45–50 (or earlier with family history), the checkup can include a prostate screening with a **PSA** blood test. If there's constant fatigue or low energy, **testosterone** can also be measured. ## Silent Conditions We Frequently Detect

These are the conditions we find most often in checkups of men who "felt fine":

- **High blood pressure** — we manage it as part of our [chronic conditions](/services/condiciones-cronicas) care, with a treatment plan and follow-up.
- **Pre-diabetes and type 2 diabetes** — caught early, they can often be controlled with diet, exercise and simple medication.

## Recommended Screenings by Age

Use this table as a general guide. At your visit, the doctor adjusts the recommendations based on your personal and family history:

| Age | Recommended screenings | Frequency |
|-----|-----------------------|-----------|
| 18–39 | Blood pressure, weight, baseline glucose and cholesterol, STI testing if sexually active | Checkup every 1–2 years |
| 40–49 | All of the above + annual glucose and cholesterol, baseline electrocardiogram, PSA if family history | Annual checkup |
| 50–64 | All of the above + PSA and prostate exam, full cardiovascular evaluation | Annual checkup |
| 65+ | Complete checkup with kidney function, heart, prostate and a review of all your medications | Annual or as directed |

If you've never had a checkup, the best age to start is the one you are now. Your first checkup creates your baseline: the numbers you start with, and what needs watching.

## Warning Signs You Should Not Ignore

Between checkups, get evaluated as soon as possible if you notice:

- Chest pain or pressure, especially with exertion
- Excessive thirst and needing to urinate constantly
- Weight loss without dieting
- Fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Difficulty urinating, a weak stream, or getting up several times at night
- Swelling in the legs or ankles
- Frequent headaches
- Changes in sexual function, which can be an early sign of circulation problems

None of these signs means something serious on its own, but every one of them deserves a medical evaluation instead of "waiting to see if it goes away."

## Practical Tips Between Checkups

The checkup happens once a year; your health is built every day:

1. Walk at least 30 minutes a day — no gym required.
2. Cut back on sodas and sugary drinks; they're the easiest sugar source to trim.
3. Go easy on salt and ultra-processed foods to protect your blood pressure.
4. Sleep 7–8 hours: poor sleep raises both blood pressure and blood sugar.
5. If you smoke, ask for help quitting — it's the single change with the biggest benefit for your heart.
6. Know your numbers: blood pressure, glucose and cholesterol. What gets measured can be improved.
